<p><p>Final expense insurance can be categorized into two market types: permanent and non-permanent. The permanent market includes whole life policies, offering lifelong coverage with a guaranteed death benefit and cash value accumulation. This type ensures that expenses are covered regardless of when they occur. In contrast, the non-permanent market consists of term insurance, which provides coverage for a specified period. It's generally more affordable but requires beneficiaries to secure other options if the insured passes away after the term ends.</p></p>
