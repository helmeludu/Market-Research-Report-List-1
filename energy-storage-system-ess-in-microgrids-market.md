<p><p>Energy Storage Systems (ESS) in microgrids can be classified into two main market types: Grid-Tied and Independent. </p><p>Grid-Tied ESS connects to the larger electric grid, allowing for energy exchange, grid services, and enhanced reliability during peak demands or outages. Conversely, Independent ESS operates autonomously, generating and storing energy for localized use without reliance on external grids. This setup is particularly beneficial for remote areas or during emergencies, fostering energy independence and resilience in microgrid applications.</p></p>
